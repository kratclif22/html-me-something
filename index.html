Forensics 101

Mechanics of a Tournament

  • Rounds

    • Each Event consists of two to three preliminary rounds of six to eight competitors in each event and one to two judges (depending on tournament size). Each competitor presents their piece for the judges. The judges then rank each competitor in that round based on the number of total competitors.

  • Scoring

    • Forensics (or Speech) is like golf: the lower the score the better. Scores for each round per each Event are tabulated and after all the preliminary rounds are complete, each competitor is given a final score to determine if they qualify for the Final round in each Event.

  • Finals

    • The six to eight competitors (depending on ties in preliminary scoring) perform a final round for three judges and their peers. The judges then rank these Finalists and rankings are announced at the Award Ceremony.

  • Awards

    • A ceremonious event at the end of the tournament after all final rounds are completed and tabulated. The finalists from each Event are presented an award based on final rank starting from sixth place leading up to first place.

Events

  • Interpretation Events

    • Interp Events are 8 minutes in length and can be cut from any published source. Most often plays and books are used, but movies or poems can be used as well. The content should be concise and easy for the audience to follow. These are sometimes referred to as "acting" events.

  • Impromptu Events

    • Impromptu Events are just as they sound: on-the-spot speeches prepared during the round or, in the case of Extemporaneous Speaking, in a 45 minute window before each round. The competitors are given a topic based on the event. Extemp focuses on current world affairs; Impromptu topics can be almost anything from Mango to Mother Theresa to Identity Politics; Radio Speaking is a mock radio broadcast formulated with the basics of a radio show: news, weather, sports, adverts.

  • Traditional Oratory

    • These events are what most people think of when you say "I am giving a speech". These events are 8 minutes in lenght and cover a range of styles and frameworks. Original Oratory is a persuasive speech on any topic; Declaration Speaking is a paraphrased speech from a past source such as Obama's 2008 inagural address; Special Occasion Speaking is a comical speech that focuses on persuasion.

Judging

  • Critique

    • The judges in each round rank AND critique each competitor. The critique should be helpful to the competitor. Often, judges are asked to continue to judge tournaments throughout the year and are paid by the school from which they are hired. If the judge consistently gives negative and demoralizing feedback to the competitors, the judge likely not be asked to return. A good judge is impartial and objective driven.

  • Ranking

    • Rankings are given in each round and determine the total score of each competitor. Rankings can sometimes greatly differ from judge to judge. A competitor might be ranked 1,1,6 - this phenomenon is called "squirreling". Judges who consistently "squirrel" competitors are considered unfit and will not be asked to return.

  • Aptitude

    • Judges should have a good understanding of the events they are judging. Judges often judge the same events from tournament to tournament, but never the same event in each tournament. Judges should be objective focused and want each competitor to succeed and learn from their experience.

Coaching

  • Experience

    • Coaching gives the competitors a chance to present their piece to their coaches and to their peers. Coaching is a way for competitors to practice in front of a live audience and receive good, honest feedback to allow their piece and performance to grow.

  • Repetition

    • Repetition is a necessary tool used by competitors to help them memorize their piece. Almost all of the Events require that the competitor be memorized and perform without stumbles or memory lapses.

  • Empathy

    • Coaching can be exhausting because at times, competitors get stuck in the same routine of delivery. Coaching MUST be a compassionate experince for both the competitor and the coach in order to avoid burn out.

Take Aways

  • #1 Fear

    • Public speaking is often listed as the #1 Fear of Americans today. Speech gives students a chance to overcome this fear before their college and professional lives begin.

  • Relationships

    • The relationships developed during the Speech writing and speech giving process are invaluable and contribute greatly to the growth of students before they leave High School.

  • Practice Practice Practice

    • Speech gives students the opportunity to learn the value of practice. Practice makes perfect and helps students understand the cycle of speech writing and giving.

  • Memory

    • Anecdotally, my memory is actually really good! I attribute my ablilty to remember names and faces with my years devoted to Speech.

  • Professional Applications

    • Speech is a great way to get students started on their professional skills before they are even in the professional job market. Presenting confidently, being prepared for tasks in advance, memorizing information, talking to and sharing information with unfamiliar people, knowing how to communicate verbally in a way that is clear and concise, the list of Professional Applications can go on and on!
